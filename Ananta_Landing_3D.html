— HOW IT WORKS

A week of Ananta looks like a week of school — with better teachers and a longer attention span.

Every weekday at 7 PM, one subject is taught for an hour. Monday is Mathematics with Praketa. Tuesday is Science with Vihaan. Wednesday is Social Science with Adhvara. Thursday is Kannada with Harini. Friday is English with Anika. Saturday is Hindi with Amita. On Sunday, there's a one-hour weekly test on what was taught that week, followed by an hour of doubt clearing where the AI teachers answer questions from anyone in the cohort.

Every class is recorded the moment it ends. Notes are generated automatically — summary, key formulas, diagrams — and saved to the student's dashboard. A short quiz appears right after the class to reinforce what was just taught. Anything the student gets wrong becomes a flashcard in the Memory Deck, which surfaces it again a few days later on a spaced-repetition schedule.

SCHEDULING RULE

When one subject finishes early, the slot helps another

Hindi has fifteen chapters; Social Science has thirty. If Hindi wraps up its syllabus in early December, Saturday evenings don't go to waste — they automatically redirect to Social Science, which still has chapters to cover. The student dashboard shows clearly when a slot has been redirected, so nobody is confused about whose class is on tonight.

Live classes start the day the tenth student joins.

The Sunday doubt session, the weekly test rankings, the cohort feel — none of it works well with just one or two students.
