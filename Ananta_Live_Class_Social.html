Ma'am, what did the colours of the flag mean?
Adhvara
Three colours, three ideas. Blue and red were the colours of Paris; white was the colour of the king. Putting them together meant the people and the monarchy now shared power — that was the new France. Liberté, égalité, fraternité.
MN
Meena · 7:25 PM
how is this connected to India ma'am?
Adhvara
Read our Constitution's Preamble — "we, the people…" and "liberty, equality, fraternity." Those exact words come from the French Revolution. Tilak, Gandhi, Nehru all read these texts in school. The idea that a government must answer to its people travelled from Paris in 1789 to India over the next 150 years.
