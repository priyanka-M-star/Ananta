Chemical Reactions — Class Notes

Generated from today's live class by Vihaan · Science

1. What is a chemical reaction?

A chemical reaction is a process in which one or more substances (reactants) are converted into one or more different substances (products). It involves the breaking of old bonds and the formation of new ones.

Key Idea

Atoms are conserved — they only rearrange. Mass before = mass after.

Remember

Look for clues: colour change, gas, precipitate, heat, light.

2. Today's worked example

Burning magnesium ribbon in air produces a bright white flame and a white powder.

2 Mg + O2 → 2 MgO + heat + light

3. Types of reactions

  • ACombination: two or more reactants form a single product. Example: CaO + H₂O → Ca(OH)₂.
  • BDecomposition: a single reactant breaks down. Example: 2 H₂O → 2 H₂ + O₂ (electrolysis).
  • CDisplacement: a more reactive element displaces a less reactive one.
  • DDouble displacement: ions are exchanged between two compounds.
  • ERedox: involves simultaneous oxidation and reduction.

4. Balancing equations — 3-step method

  1. Write the skeletal equation.
  2. Count atoms of each element on both sides.
  3. Adjust coefficients (never subscripts) until atoms match.
